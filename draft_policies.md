## 2. Conflicts of Interest Policy

*Suggested URL: `/conflicts-of-interest`*

Macaulay Consulting takes the management of conflicts of interest seriously. Health and public sector clients have legitimate expectations that those they engage will act with integrity and transparency, and this policy explains how we do that.

### What we consider a conflict of interest
A conflict of interest exists where Caroline Macaulay's personal interests, professional relationships or prior engagements could reasonably be seen to compromise — or appear to compromise — her impartiality, independence or judgement in the work she does for a client.

This includes:

- Concurrent or recent work for organisations whose interests are directly opposed to a prospective client
- Personal relationships with people likely to be affected by a piece of work
- Financial interests in organisations or programmes connected to a piece of work
- Any other circumstance a reasonable observer might view as a conflict

### How we manage conflicts

1. **Before accepting work**, Caroline reviews each prospective engagement against current and recent commitments and discloses any actual, potential or perceived conflicts to the client in writing.
2. **During an engagement**, if a new conflict emerges, Caroline discloses it immediately and works with the client to determine an appropriate course of action.
3. **Where a conflict cannot be managed**, Caroline declines the work or withdraws from the engagement.

### Public sector procurement
For engagements with Queensland Government and other public sector clients, Caroline is familiar with sector-specific conflict-of-interest declaration requirements and will complete any required forms as part of the engagement process.

### Questions
If you are considering engaging Macaulay Consulting and have questions about how conflicts of interest are managed, please contact Caroline directly.

## 3. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Commitment

*Suggested URL: `/confidentiality`*

Facilitation, strategy and culture work involves people sharing things they might not say in other contexts. Senior leaders speak honestly about their organisations. Staff share difficult experiences. Stakeholders raise concerns they have not raised elsewhere. The quality of the work depends entirely on people feeling safe enough to be candid.

This commitment explains how Macaulay Consulting treats what is shared in that work.

### What is confidential
By default, everything is confidential. This includes:

- The content of discussions, workshops and interviews
- Documents shared by the client or its stakeholders
- Identities of participants in workshops and interviews, where individual contributions are attributed
- The fact of an engagement itself, unless the client agrees it can be referenced

Outputs of an engagement — strategies, plans, evaluation findings — are the client's property and the client decides what is shared and with whom.

### How information is handled

- **Storage:** Working files are kept in secured, password-protected cloud storage. Original documents are returned or deleted at the end of an engagement at the client's request.
- **Notes:** Caroline keeps facilitation notes only where they are useful for delivering the engagement. They are not shared beyond the engagement team without explicit client consent.
- **Reporting:** When findings are shared, they are aggregated and de-identified unless the client has agreed otherwise.

### Limits to confidentiality
Confidentiality applies except where:

- The client gives written consent to share specific information
- Disclosure is required by law (for example, where there is a serious risk to a person's safety, or where compelled by court order)
- Information is already in the public domain through no fault of Macaulay Consulting

### Use in case studies and marketing
Engagements are only referenced in marketing material — including case studies on this website — with the client's written permission. Where permission is given, the client has final approval over the wording before publication.

### Sub-contracting
Caroline Macaulay does all client-facing work personally. In rare cases where a specialist is brought in (for example, a co-facilitator for a large workshop), they are bound by the same confidentiality commitments before any client information is shared with them.

## 4. Independence and Impartiality Statement

*Suggested URL: `/independence`*

Facilitation, co-design and qualitative evaluation only work when the practitioner is — and is seen to be — independent of the outcome. This statement describes how Macaulay Consulting maintains that independence.

### Independence in practice

- **No stake in the outcome.** Caroline has no financial, professional or personal interest in any particular conclusion a workshop, evaluation or planning process might reach. Her role is to enable the client and its stakeholders to reach the best decision available to them, not to steer the work toward a predetermined answer.
- **Process expertise, not content advocacy.** In facilitation engagements, Caroline brings expertise in process: how to structure a conversation, how to surface different perspectives, how to move a group toward action. The content — the specifics of the organisation's strategy, services or culture — is the client's.
- **Honest reporting.** In evaluation work, findings reflect what was actually heard and observed, including findings that may be uncomfortable for the commissioning organisation.

### Disclosing prior relationships
Where Caroline has prior professional relationships with the client, its stakeholders or its sector, she discloses these at the outset so the client can decide whether they affect the suitability of the engagement.

### Speaking up
If during an engagement Caroline forms a view that the work is being shaped in ways that compromise its integrity — for example, that a workshop is being used to validate a decision already made, or that evaluation findings are being filtered — she will raise this with the client directly. Where it cannot be resolved, she will withdraw from the engagement.

### Why this matters
For health and public sector clients in particular, the credibility of a process often depends on the independence of the facilitator or evaluator. This statement is intended to give clients — and the participants in their engagements — confidence in that independence.

## 5. Engagement Terms

*Suggested URL: `/engagement-terms` — and referenced from each proposal/contract*

These terms apply to all engagements with Macaulay Consulting unless varied in writing between the parties.

**Note: this is a starting-point draft. Engagement Terms should be reviewed by a lawyer before being relied on, particularly the liability, IP and termination clauses.**

### 1. Scope and deliverables
Each engagement is documented in a written proposal or scoping note that sets out:

- The purpose and intended outcomes of the work
- The activities Macaulay Consulting will deliver
- Timeframes and key milestones
- Any inputs required from the client
- Fees and payment terms

Changes to scope are agreed in writing before any additional work is undertaken.

### 2. Fees and payment
- Fees are quoted in Australian dollars, exclusive of GST unless stated otherwise.
- Invoices are issued at agreed milestones or monthly in arrears.
- Payment terms are 14 days from invoice date unless agreed otherwise.
- Reasonable out-of-pocket expenses (travel beyond Brisbane, venue costs, materials) are charged at cost and itemised on invoices.

### 3. Intellectual property

- **Pre-existing IP** — frameworks, methods and materials Caroline brings to the engagement remain her property and may be used in other engagements.
- **Engagement-specific work product** — strategies, plans, evaluation reports and other documents produced specifically for the client are the client's property on full payment of fees.
- **Aggregate learning** — Caroline may draw on general insights and patterns from her work in future engagements and publications, provided no client is identified and no confidential information is disclosed.

### 4. Confidentiality
The Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Commitment published at `/confidentiality` forms part of these terms.

### 5. Cancellation and rescheduling

- More than 14 days notice: no fee.
- 7–14 days notice: 50% of the agreed fee for that activity.
- Less than 7 days notice: 100% of the agreed fee for that activity.

This reflects that workshop and facilitation dates are held exclusively for the client and cannot be easily backfilled.

### 6. Liability
To the extent permitted by law, Macaulay Consulting's total liability under any engagement is limited to the fees paid by the client for that engagement. Macaulay Consulting maintains professional indemnity and public liability insurance appropriate to the work it undertakes.

### 7. Termination
Either party may terminate an engagement on 14 days written notice. On termination:

- The client pays for work performed to the termination date.
- Macaulay Consulting provides the client with all work product completed to that point.
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination.

### 8. Governing law
These terms are governed by the laws of Queensland, Australia. Any disputes that cannot be resolved through good-faith discussion will be referred to mediation before any other proceedings.

### 9. Variation
These terms may be varied by written agreement between the parties.

## Practical suggestions for Caroline

A few thoughts on how these could be used:

1. **Get the Privacy Policy and Website Terms up first.** These are the legal baseline and should be in place before the site goes public.

2. **Conflicts, Confidentiality and Independence are differentiators.** Most consultants don't publish these. Doing so is itself a trust signal — it shows you're thinking like someone who understands the sector you work in.

3. **Engagement Terms don't necessarily need to live on the public website.** They can be a Documents page download, or simply attached to every proposal. Public visibility is less important than consistent application.

4. **Consider footer placement** for the policy links — small but persistent, similar to how most professional services firms handle it.

5. **Review cadence:** all of these should be dated and reviewed annually, with the review date shown at the bottom (e.g. "Last reviewed: May 2026").
