### Biography

**Photo:** `assets/images/portrait.webp` (live)

Caroline Macaulay spent fifteen years as a speech pathologist, working with children, young people and families to help them find and use their voice. That experience seeded everything that followed: a deep belief in the power of connection, a commitment to listening before acting, and a conviction that systems should empower people rather than diminish them.

Her desire to improve how essential services are designed and delivered led her to a second career in health strategy, where she spent years co-designing and implementing human-centred services and programmes at scale across Queensland.

In 2023, Caroline established Macaulay Consulting to bring that experience directly to organisations navigating change. She draws on a background in strategy, planning, service improvement, innovation and leadership, combined with qualifications in neuro-leadership, emotional culture and psychological safety, to create conditions where real change becomes possible.

Her work spans Queensland Health, West Moreton Health, Children's Health Queensland, the Department of the Premier and Cabinet, yourtown, Brisbane South PHN, the Lung Foundation, and a number of other health and public sector organisations.

### What it is
**Eyebrow:** What it is

**Heading:** More than team-building.

Most teams don't lack goodwill. What they often lack is a shared language for talking about how they work together, and a structured way to do something about it.

More Joy at Work gives teams both. Drawing on emotional culture research, neuro-leadership frameworks and Caroline's experience working with dozens of teams across the health and public sector, the programme helps people understand what shapes their experience at work and take practical steps to improve it.

This is not a one-off team-building day. It is a considered approach to culture that meets teams where they are and helps them move, together, toward something better.

**Link:** See all services from Macaulay Consulting → /services

---

### The programme
**Eyebrow:** The programme

**Heading:** What it involves

`[CAROLINE TO CONFIRM — please review the phase names, sequence and descriptions below. These are based on the Emotional Culture Deck methodology and existing site content. Expand, rename or restructure to match how you actually deliver the programme.]`

**Phase 1 — Culture Assessment**
The team explores how they currently feel about their work and what is shaping that experience. This draws on validated emotional culture tools and is facilitated with care and psychological safety.

**Phase 2 — Shared Language**
The team develops a common understanding of the emotions and values that matter to them, and begins to articulate the culture they want to build.

**Phase 3 — Practical Action**
The team identifies specific, manageable commitments they can make to one another. These are grounded in daily work, not aspirational statements.

*The programme is available as a standalone engagement or as part of a broader organisational change process.*
